Welcome to my page!


I am a researcher in human-technology interaction, specializing in designing and conducting studies to understand users, capturing design requirements, creating and evaluating prototypes, and evaluating the usability of software systems. I completed my PhD in HCI at the University of Glasgow, during which I investigated the use of voice-enabled smart assistants, and the design of speech interfaces for people with cognitive impairments due to acquired brain injury.

I am passionate about new, innovative technologies, and always like to come up with ideas for applications and features. I enjoy trying to empathize with users to understand their pain points, and methodically analyze user data to come up with solutions. Due to my background in programming I have a good understanding of the software development lifecycle, and can speak the right language to effectively collaborate with software engineers to convey design requirements. Additionally, my experience in academia has helped me develop good communication, presentation and team-work skills. I also have a strong interest in technology accessibility and inclusive design.

Besides research and design, I have a passion about music. Apart from playing and composing I am interested in understanding the cognitive science of what makes music so enjoyable and personal.
